Canonical bio

Cole Calfee is a Southern artist who divides his work evenly between fine art, writing, and creative direction. His practice explores presence, slowness, and meaning through visual art, long-form essays, and narrative-driven projects.

Working across painting, drawing, and written form, Calfee is interested in how attention is shaped—by place, by technology, and by the stories we choose to live inside. His work favors clarity over noise and continuity over trend.

He is the founder of Haven, an ongoing exploration of alignment, attention, and modern life, and the creative force behind Tangerine, a narrative studio rooted in Southern sensibility and cinematic storytelling. His creative direction work is carried out through Coleman VII, a studio practice extending his artistic values into collaborative projects.

Across disciplines, the approach is monklike and deliberate—alive, but unhurried.

Based in the American South.
